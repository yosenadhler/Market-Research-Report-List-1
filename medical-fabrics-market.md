<p><ul><li>Non-woven</li><li>Woven</li><li>Knitted</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical fabrics market comprises three primary types: non-woven, woven, and knitted fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are made by bonding fibers through chemical, mechanical, or thermal processes, offering high absorbency and filtration, ideal for surgical gowns and masks. Woven fabrics, created by interlacing threads, provide strength and durability, commonly used in medical uniforms and bedding. Knitted fabrics are formed by interlooping yarn, resulting in flexibility and comfort, suitable for items like patient garments and specialized supports. Each type serves distinct medical applications.</p></p>
