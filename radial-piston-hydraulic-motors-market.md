<p><p>Radial piston hydraulic motors are classified into two primary types based on displacement: multiple displacements and single displacement. </p><p>Multiple displacement motors feature adjustable displacement, allowing operators to change the motor's output by altering the volume of fluid delivered to the pistons, providing flexibility for various applications. In contrast, single displacement motors have a fixed displacement, offering consistent performance but less adaptability. The choice between these types depends on the application's demands, such as torque needs and speed variations.</p></p>
