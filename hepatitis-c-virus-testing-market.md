<p><p>Hepatitis C Virus (HCV) testing encompasses various methodologies, primarily divided into antibody tests and nucleic acid tests. Antibody tests detect the presence of antibodies generated in response to HCV infection, indicating exposure to the virus. Nucleic Acid Tests (NAT) directly identify the presence of HCV RNA, confirming active infection and assessing viral load. Other testing methods may include genotyping for virus strain identification or liver function tests to evaluate potential liver damage, providing comprehensive insights into HCV infection management.</p></p>
