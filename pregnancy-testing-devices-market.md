<p><p>Pregnancy testing devices primarily include blood tests and urine tests for detecting human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG). Blood tests are performed in clinical settings and can detect lower levels of hCG, offering early pregnancy detection and quantitative results. Urine tests, commonly found in home kits, are user-friendly and provide quick results, but are typically less sensitive than blood tests. Both methods play vital roles in confirming pregnancy, catering to different preferences for accuracy, convenience, and accessibility.</p></p>
