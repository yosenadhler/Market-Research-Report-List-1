<p><p>The self-reconfiguring modular robot market consists of three main types: chain architecture, lattice architecture, and hybrid architecture. Chain architecture enables robots to connect in a linear formation, providing flexibility in movement and task execution. Lattice architecture involves a grid-like structure, allowing for complex shape formations and stable configurations. Hybrid architecture combines elements of both chain and lattice designs, optimizing versatility and functionality by adapting to various environments and tasks, making it suitable for diverse applications in robotics.</p></p>
