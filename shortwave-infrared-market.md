<p><p>The shortwave infrared (SWIR) market encompasses two primary types: cooled and uncooled technologies. Cooled SWIR detectors utilize a cryogenic cooling system to enhance sensitivity and detect weak infrared signals, making them suitable for demanding applications like scientific research and high-end surveillance. In contrast, uncooled SWIR detectors operate at ambient temperatures, offering a more cost-effective solution with lower sensitivity, suitable for applications such as industrial inspections and basic surveillance. Both types serve diverse industries, leveraging their unique advantages for specific use cases.</p></p>
