<p><ul><li>Normal Pump</li><li>Patch Pump</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The portable insulin pump market includes two main types: normal pumps and patch pumps. Normal pumps are typically worn on a belt or in a pocket, delivering insulin through a small cannula, allowing for precise glucose control. Patch pumps, on the other hand, are adhesive devices that attach directly to the skin and are more discreet, often providing a tubeless experience. Both types offer continuous insulin delivery, enhancing convenience and management for individuals with diabetes.</p></p>
