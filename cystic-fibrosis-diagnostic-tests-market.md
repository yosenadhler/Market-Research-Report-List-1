<p><strong>The Cystic Fibrosis Diagnostic Tests Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Genetic Tests</li><li>Fecal Test</li><li>Immunoreactive Trypsinogen (IRT) Test</li><li>Sweat Chloride Tests</li><li>Pulmonary Function Tests</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cystic Fibrosis diagnostic tests encompass various methodologies. Genetic tests identify CF mutations, aiding in carrier screening. Fecal tests assess pancreatic function through stool analysis. The Immunoreactive Trypsinogen (IRT) test measures elevated IRT levels in newborns, indicating CF risk. Sweat chloride tests determine salt concentration in sweat, confirming diagnosis. Pulmonary function tests evaluate lung function, tracking disease progression. Together, these tests provide crucial insights for accurate diagnosis and management of cystic fibrosis, influencing treatment decisions and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
